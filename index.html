Muon-to-Electron Experiment

Undergraduate Reasearch Assistant/Lead Developer

I'm working as the lead developer for the Mu2e Experiment at the School of Physics and Astronomy, University of Minnesota. The goal is to create a particle accelerator to detect particles beyond the standard model. More than 40 institutions are involved and the first beam commissioning is expected to start in 2020 at Muon Campus, Fermilab. My contribution includes:
• Assemble and test components for detector to be placed in particle accelerator at Fermilab, Chicago. A $300M particle physics research project funded by United States Department of Energy to study Muon-to-Electron Conversion.
• Designed a set of GUIs with Python to help create the panels for the detector. The GUIs made sure that standard procedures were followed to make the panels with precision and perfection. The data from these procedures was collected in SQLite.
• Analyzed the collected data for hundreds of panels using SQL to make the detector efficient.

SAVIOURS: A NGO

Bird Treatment Camp

It was Jaipur’s famous kite flying festival, Makar sakranti, 2010. A bird injured by flying manjhas, that invaded its territory of flying, fell right in front of me. I could not save the bird, but I swore right there not to ever fly a kite again in my life.
I joined Saviours, a NGO aimed at saving birds and snakes. Since then, for 5 straight years, I have organized a bird treatment camp with them. We save 80+ injured birds every year during the festival. Not only do I rescue and help treat the birds, I am also responsible for management and logistics of the initiative.
